Example of a sysrematic review and meta-analysis

In this systematic review and mate-analysis, we are trying to identify the true prevalence of COVID-associated Acute Kidney Injury. Yes, you heard me correct. Once of the common complication of COVID among hospitalised patients is Acute Kidney Injury (AKI), which is a sudden loss of kidney function measured by the change in serum creatinine.

The reason driving this pice of meta-analysis was that when I was looking up the AKI prevalence among the published literatures, this number varies from 1% to 70%. Of course the AKI prevalence would be different based on different clinical setting. i.e. ICU vs non-ICU, different COVID management policy such as straight lockdown vs Herd immunity. Regardless, we want to provide a global overview of the COVID-19 AKI prevalence reported in published studies to uncover geographic and socioeconomic disparities.

In order to do that, we extracted data from more than 300 publihsed papers using Covidence.

Design of metaextractoR

Given the labour intensive process for data extraction, to remove some of the labour work, we developed this R package, metaextractoR that embeded Large Language Models to help with data extraction.

The design of this workflow follows the Cochrance systematic review guideline. At each step, there is a human-in-the-loop process incorprated.

There are three shinyapps that provide user-friendly interface for anyone to extract data using LLMs.
